LLM-based Investment Advisor Agents
for Asset Allocation in the Chinese Market

Task Description

This task evaluates the ability of LLM-based Investment Advisor Agents to perform complex reasoning and quantitative decision-making in the Chinese capital market. Participants develop agents that interpret daily macroeconomic signals, financial news, and historical price data to generate daily asset allocation strategies. All submissions are evaluated in a standardized backtesting environment with transaction cost simulation.

Track 1: Macro-Asset Allocation

Evaluate macro-inference capabilities by rebalancing across major asset classes to navigate economic cycles.

~11 macro-category ETFs

Track 2: Sector-Rotation Allocation

Focus on sensitivity to industrial policies and trends, requiring tactical adjustments across industries.

~14 industry-themed ETFs

📊 Evaluation Metrics

Primary: Sharpe Ratio Cumulative Returns Maximum Drawdown

FAQ & Submission Notes

Registration

Formal registration is required for Task 4. Please download the registration form, complete it, and send it to the task contact email. The registration deadline is May 25, 2026.

Participant Group

An official WeChat group is available for registered teams. After receiving the completed registration form, the organizing committee will invite the team to join the group.

A-list and B-list Evaluation

The shared task schedule follows the official NLPCC dates page. Because Task 4 uses both A-list and B-list evaluations and agent execution may take a long time, we have released the complete A-list data for 20250101-20251231. The B-list test data is kept private; organizers will run submitted agents on the secret B-list data for the final official ranking.

Submission Window

All teams should submit A-list results and full code between June 11 and June 20, 2026. Models, extra datasets, and knowledge bases used by the system must be limited to resources available before 2026.

Final Materials

The final evaluation submission should include full code, a runnable agent, A-list prediction files, and intermediate logs. Detailed packaging instructions will be announced through the repository and participant group.

Conference and OpenReview Paper Deadline

The paper deadline around May 26, 2026 shown on the conference page or OpenReview is not the shared task deadline. Shared task system report and paper arrangements will come later, so participants do not need to follow that deadline for Task 4 materials.
